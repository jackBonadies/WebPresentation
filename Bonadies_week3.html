Alexa's Origins

  • Alexa was founded in 1996 to generate and analyze web traffic data
  • Since the company's inception it has provided a set of extensions and toolbars in order to both collect web traffic (in the form of page visits, time spent, hyperlink path) and display site statistics (traffic, rank, authority)
  • Gathering data and analyzing data has only grown as fields such as machine learning have proven successful though requiring large amounts of data.

Relationship with the Web in the Past

  • AlexaRank has been one of the most popular page ranking methods since its inception
  • It has allowed users to quickly evaluate websites based on their rankings (and thus determine their credibility and worth, especially useful for those in the market to purchase a site/domain)
  • These rankings are based on the last 3 months of aggregated web traffic from an estimated 10 million toolbar users and uses a proprietary algorithm to combine page views and unique visitors
  • It can also assist users in determining how their traffic is changing by comparing their metric with the past metric 3 months ago

Relationship with the Web Now

  • Fields such as Machine Learning and techniques such as Search Engine Optimization are only growing. Web traffic data can be used to fuel both of these areas.
  • The number of web users is ever increasing, however, the number of users with the Alexa toolbar is estimated to be very small, only around 10 million.
  • For smaller sites, this small sample size of Alexa toolbar users may not be representative
  • The free option for Alexa is very limited. In addition there are now several competitors such as SimilarWeb, Quantcast, and Traffic Estimate. All of these estimate website traffic, providing the same set (or more) of metrics Alexa provides
  • Alternatively, users may want to focus more on Google PageRank, though this is not a rank of traffic, it is highly correlated. The better the PageRank, the more links to the page and the higher up in the Google Search results

Relationship with the Web in the Future

  • According to Alexa's own site, the traffic to Alexa has gone down steadily in the past 6 months.
  • Due to the increase in spoofing and phishing attacks, as discussed in the previous article, it may be useful for Alexa to allow those with the Alexa toolbar to flag sites.
  • Flagging in addition to popularity (facebook will be much more popular than faceb00k) may provide a metric of 'trust' for the user.
  • In this way the web will still be decentralized and anonymous while protecting against the worst excesses these two virtues tend towards (security and cybercrime).
